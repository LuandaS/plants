Indoor Plants

Beginner plants are a great way to start your gardening journey. Plants like succulents, spider plants, and pothos are easy to care for and can thrive in a variety of environments. They are perfect for those who are new to gardening or have limited space, and can add a touch of green to any room!

Succulents

Succulents are plants that have thick, fleshy tissues adapted to water storage. They are known for their ability to retain water and thrive in habitats too dry for most other plants. Succulents come in a variety of shapes, sizes, and colors, and can be grown both indoors and outdoors. Some popular indoor succulents include the panda plant, desert rose echeveria, and haworthia, while popular outdoor succulents include the agave, aloe vera, and jade plant.

Golden Pothos

Golden pothos, also known as Epipremnum aureum, is a hardy indoor foliage plant native to southeastern Asia. It is commonly grown as a hanging plant due to its ability to climb by means of aerial roots. The plant has thick, waxy, green, heart-shaped leaves with splashes of yellow, making it a beautiful trailing plant. Pothos is one of the easiest plants to grow, tolerating low light and minimal watering, making it a classic starter plant for new growers!

Beginner Tips!

  1. Choose the right plant for your space. Consider the amount of light your space receives and choose a plant that can thrive in that environment.
  2. Provide adequate light. Most indoor plants require bright, indirect light to grow well. If natural light is not possible, consider using artificial plant lights.
  3. Water your plants properly. Overwatering or underwatering can harm your plants. Make sure to water your plants when the soil is dry to the touch, and avoid letting them sit in standing water.
  4. Fertilize your plants. Indoor plants need nutrients to grow, so fertilize them regularly with a balanced fertilizer. Be careful not to over-fertilize, as this can damage your plants.
